Introduction

The rapid adoption of AI tools in education creates both opportunities and risks. This assignment argues that institutions should adopt AI-assisted feedback systems when they are transparent, evidence-grounded, and supervised by instructors.

Methodology

To support this argument, I reviewed three case studies from higher-education programs that piloted formative AI feedback. In each case, students received earlier feedback and revised drafts more frequently than comparison groups.

Analysis

The first case study reported a 19% increase in rubric alignment after introducing structured AI hints. The second found improved writing confidence but also highlighted bias concerns when prompts were vague. The third emphasized instructor oversight as essential for quality control.

Conclusion

AI feedback should not replace teachers, but it can improve consistency and turnaround time. The strongest implementation model combines rubric-based AI support with instructor review and clear student guidance.
